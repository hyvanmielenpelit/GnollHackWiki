> 👉 **A diluted potion is a weaker version of a standard potion, often resulting from dipping a potion into water. Diluted potions generally have reduced effects, shortened durations, and provide half the nutrition of regular potions.**

## Overview

In GnollHack, a potion can acquire the `diluted` property, which represents it being watered down. This makes its effects significantly less potent and reliable.

## General Effects of Dilution

When you quaff any diluted potion, the following general rules apply compared to an undiluted potion:

- **Halved Healing and Damage**: The primary effect magnitude (such as the amount of hit points healed or damage taken) is reduced by half.
- **Halved Duration**: Any temporary status effects (such as levitation, blindness, invisibility, or confusion) will last for only half the normal duration.
- **Halved Nutrition**: A diluted potion provides exactly half the satiety (nutrition) of its undiluted counterpart.

## Specific Potion Effects

Beyond the general reductions, dilution alters the specific behavior of many potions. The table below details these specific changes when quaffing or interacting with them.

| Potion | Effect of Dilution |
| :--- | :--- |
| [[/Items/Potion of blindness]] | The blindness effect lasts for half the normal duration. |
| [[/Items/Potion of dwarven mushroom brew]] | Quaffing it will refresh you without causing confusion. (Undiluted brew confuses the player.) |
| [[/Items/Potion of elven herbal brew]] | Does not provide the small 1 HP heal that the undiluted brew gives. |
| [[/Items/Potion of gain ability]] | There is a 50% chance that the potion will have no effect at all. |
| [[/Items/Potion of gain level]] | There is a 50% chance that the potion will fail, giving only "an elevated feeling" (or an "uneasy feeling" if cursed) instead of a level up or rising to the level above. |
| [[/Items/Potion of healing]] <br> [[/Items/Potion of extra-healing]] <br> [[/Items/Potion of greater healing]] <br> [[/Items/Potion of full healing]] | Heals half the normal amount of hit points. If the potion is breathed or splashed on you, there is a 50% chance it will fail to increase your maximum HP. |
| [[/Items/Potion of lightning speed]] <br> [[/Items/Potion of greater speed]] <br> [[/Items/Potion of speed]] | The speed buff is stepped down by one tier. For example, a diluted potion of lightning speed grants the same buff as an undiluted potion of greater speed. |
| [[/Items/Potion of oil]] | When used to refill a light source, it provides significantly less fuel than an undiluted potion. |
| [[/Items/Potion of polymorph]] | There is a 50% chance that the potion will fail, causing you to only "shudder for a moment" instead of changing your form. |
| [[/Items/Potion of restore ability]] | Even if blessed, it will only restore a single drained attribute. (An undiluted blessed potion restores all drained attributes.) |
| [[/Items/Potion of see invisible]] | A blessed diluted potion only grants temporary see invisible. (An undiluted blessed potion grants the ability permanently.) |
| [[/Items/Potion of sickness]] | Acts as a mildly stale potion instead of being biologically contaminated. This significantly reduces the damage taken and prevents food poisoning. |

## Other Uses

- **Dipping in Water**: Dipping an already diluted potion into water or a fountain usually washes it out completely, turning it into a [[/Items/potion of water]]. Dipping a standard potion into water usually makes it diluted.
